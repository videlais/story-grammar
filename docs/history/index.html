📜 A History of Combinatorial Grammars

Print Experiments & Digital Projects, 1850–2026

How writers, mathematicians, and programmers turned recombination into a literary form.

Combinatorial literature treats writing as a system: a small set of parts that can be permuted into a vast number of texts. The modern grammar generators that power tools like Story Grammar descend from a long lineage — from permutational verse and mechanical print toys, through avant-garde chance procedures, formal mathematical constraints, and finally software. The pages below trace that lineage across four eras, each with academic references and citations.

1850–1899 · Mechanical Verse
Permutational print, proteus poems, the chance of the page, and the prehistory of the algorithm.
1900–1949 · Chance & Cut-Up
Dada poems, exquisite corpses, Markov chains, and Shannon's information theory.
1950–1999 · Oulipo & the Computer
Queneau's hundred thousand billion poems, Strachey's love letters, and computer poetry.
2000–2026 · The Open Web
Tracery, NaNoGenMo, bots, and grammar engines — combinatorics for everyone.
